# User guide

A walkthrough of CVMatch from the perspective of someone using it to check how well their resume fits a job.

---

## 1. Sign in

Open the app at `http://localhost:3000` and click **Get Started** in the top-right. Clerk handles sign-up/sign-in with email or a social provider.

If you skip sign-in, the analyzer page is gated by a paywall component and you won't be able to run a match.

---

## 2. Open the analyzer

Click **Analyze** in the navbar, or go directly to `/analyzer`.

You'll see two inputs:

- **CV upload** — PDF, DOCX, or TXT. Max 5 MB.
- **Job description** — free-text box. Paste the full posting.

---

## 3. Submit

Click **Analyze Compatibility**. A pipeline indicator appears on the right showing six stages:

1. **Extract & parse CV** — read the file, extract text
2. **Normalize CV** — GPT reformats the structured CV into embedding-ready fields
3. **Preprocess JD** — GPT extracts required/preferred skills, summary, key phrases
4. **Embed CV sections** — skills, experience, education, overall
5. **Embed JD sections** — same sections for the job
6. **Match & score** — Jaccard + fuzzy + semantic skill matching, cosine similarity, sigmoid calibration

Each step lights up as it runs. Typical total time: 5–15 seconds (cached CVs are faster).

---

## 4. Read the results

### The big number

A donut chart shows your match percentage (0–100%) and a rating band:

| Range | Rating | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 90–100% | Excellent Match | Strong candidate — definitely apply |
| 75–90% | Good Match | Good candidate — apply with confidence |
| 60–75% | Moderate Match | Consider applying — highlight transferable skills |
| 40–60% | Weak Match | Significant gaps — apply cautiously |
| 0–40% | Poor Match | Not recommended — major misalignment |

### Score breakdown

Four tiles show each sub-score:

- **Overall** — full-CV ↔ full-JD semantic similarity
- **Skills** — keyword + fuzzy + semantic match coverage
- **Experience** — section-level semantic fit + job-title overlap boost
- **Education** — section-level semantic fit + education-level boost

### "How X% was calculated"

Below the tiles, a card shows the actual math used: each signal × its weight, the weighted sum, the sigmoid step, and the final percentage. Live-updated from the real response — not a mock.

> 📖 For the full formula and every calibration step, see [`/scoring`](http://localhost:3000/scoring) in the running app or [`TECHNICAL_GUIDE.md`](./TECHNICAL_GUIDE.md).

### Skill analysis

Three chip groups:

- **Matched** — required skills the candidate has (exact, fuzzy, or semantic match)
- **Missing** — required skills not found in the CV
- **Additional** — skills the candidate has that weren't required (bonus, not penalized)

### Strengths & gaps

Auto-generated bullets highlight what's strong (top matched skills, good experience fit) and what's weak (missing skills, weak experience fit). Written in plain language.

### Summaries

- **Job summary** — a 250-word overview of the posting generated by GPT
- **Candidate summary** — a neutral summary of the CV, also GPT-generated

### Job requirements

- Required / preferred skill chips
- Experience years
- Education level
- Key phrases (bigrams/trigrams capturing the role essence)

---

## 5. Try a different JD

Change the job description box and click **Analyze Compatibility** again. The CV is cached — the second match on the same CV is much faster (no re-parsing, no re-embedding).

If you upload a different CV, the cache miss is normal and the full pipeline runs again.

---

## Tips for better scores

- **Use a text-selectable PDF**, not an image-only scan. If the extractor can't pull text, you'll see "Unable to extract text" or a very low score due to empty skills.
- **Use real skill names**, not just acronyms. GPT usually expands them (JS → JavaScript, POS → point of sale), but explicit is safer.
- **Write out responsibilities**, not just titles. The system can extract implicit skills ("managed inventory" → "inventory management") — but only if the experience is described, not just listed as a job title.
- **Paste the full job description**, not just a 1-line summary. More context = better skill extraction from the JD side.

---

## Errors you might see

| Message | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| File too large — maximum size is 5MB | CV > 5 MB | Compress or convert |
| Invalid input. Please check your CV and job description | Empty text extracted | Check your CV isn't scan-only |
| Analysis service is temporarily unavailable | Backend couldn't reach OpenAI or the call failed | Wait a moment and retry. Persistent failures usually mean a bad API key |
| Could not connect to the server | Frontend can't reach the backend | Check backend is running (`docker compose ps`) |

---

## Privacy

Your CV content is sent to OpenAI for parsing, normalization, and embedding. It is **not** stored on disk beyond the session's SQLite embedding cache (which holds vectors, not raw text). See the in-app **Privacy Policy** page for details.
